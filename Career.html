Engineer Meetings :

My Engineering School, ENSEEIHT, asked us to ask questions to some engineers to understand better the life that we will have in a bunch of years. I find the idea interessant and legitimate my shy demands for interview that I sent to them.
To begin with, I asked M. François-Xavier MOUY, System Engineer working for CNES. He did his Engineer Training in a Perpignan Engineering School after a Technician Formation. My discussion with M.MOUY was very interessant because he told me more about a sector that interessed me a lot, working for the Armement General Direction (DGA) in classified folders. I'm quite intersseted by working for one of the Security General DIrection of the country (DGSE/I) and havin his point of view on this job-type helped me to have a better understanding of that. He also describe his type-week composed of a bunch of days in Remote-work to work on mails and projects and the rest working at CNES, going from technical issue to software problem. Finally, I learned that his technician formation blocked the possibility to being in management but allowed him to go more on terrain than a standard engineer.
When you are an technical engineer, you need to follow the technology innovation.

I then asked M. Jean-François BONNEFOUS, Engineer and Project and Programm Manager working for Airbus. M.BONNEFOUS studied in Centrale Lyon, his interview was very interessant as He told me his opinion on Mobilities and Internship. For him, it is primoridal that our internship take place in a company that interssted us to verify our idea of it and create a first contact with it.Mobility seems quite important too, he diddn't have to do an international mobility during his training but that something that he regret a lot as it offer the opportunity to discover a new culture, moreover in the internationally-connected world in which we are today. M.BONNEFOUS have a more important part of management in his job, so his weeks, most seems like some fixed meetings with his teams and some desk works the rest of the time.
It is clear that your first job will be in your internship sector, so choose it wisely

I finally asked M. Franck MAMALET, senior expert in Artificial Inteligence, working for IRT Saint-Exupery. He studied at École Polytechnique and Télécom ParisTech, with internships in Japan and the UK, which influenced his career direction. He then started at CNET (now Orange Labs), working on early digital telephony and later moving into computer vision and neural networks. Now, M.MAMALET is a senior AI expert at IRT Saint Exupéry, focusing on robust AI for critical systems like aircraft. His work involves interdisciplinary collaboration and adapting to fast-paced AI advancements. He see software engineering evolving with AI, requiring broader skills for understanding and debugging increasingly complex systems. Talking with him learn mealot about the research possibilities of the Engineer Works.
